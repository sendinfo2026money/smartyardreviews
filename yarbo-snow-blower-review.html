What Is the Yarbo Snow Blower?

The Yarbo is a fully autonomous 2-stage robotic snow blower. It operates without any boundary wires or physical infrastructure — it uses RTK GPS and stereo vision cameras to navigate your property, map your driveway, and clear snow on its own schedule.

It's part of the Yarbo modular platform, which means the same base unit can also run a lawn mower module and a leaf blower module — making it potentially a year-round yard robot if you invest in the additional attachments.

❄️ What makes it different Weather-triggered autonomy is the standout feature. The Yarbo monitors weather conditions and automatically starts clearing when it detects snowfall — without you touching an app or setting a schedule. It's the closest thing to a set-it-and-forget-it snow removal system that currently exists.

Real-World Performance

The most compelling real-world test of the Yarbo came from Tom Moloughney, host of the State of Charge YouTube channel and a senior editor at InsideEVs, who documented the Yarbo clearing his New Jersey driveway during a major Nor'easter that dropped 10–19 inches from Maryland to Maine.

His experience: sitting by the fireplace watching the Olympics closing ceremonies while the Yarbo autonomously cleared the driveway. "The @yarboglobal autonomous snow blower is really awesome," he wrote on X. The robot auto-recharged mid-storm and resumed clearing without any intervention.

Across multiple reviewer accounts, these performance patterns emerge consistently:

Where the Yarbo excels

  • Daily snowfall and moderate storms (under 12 inches) — handles these extremely well, autonomously and repeatedly
  • Wet, heavy snow — the 2-stage design handles wet New England snow better than single-stage electric blowers
  • Continuous operation — auto-recharges and resumes, meaning it can clear incrementally throughout a storm rather than waiting for it to stop
  • Obstacle avoidance — RTK GPS plus stereo vision cameras reliably avoids cars, trash cans, and pets
  • Cold weather — performs reliably down to -13°F, with optional heating modules for extreme cold

Where it struggles

  • Extreme storms over 18 inches — reviewers consistently flag this as beyond the Yarbo's comfort zone
  • Plow berms — the compacted piles left at the end of your driveway by the town plow are dense and heavy, sometimes exceeding the Yarbo's capability
  • Large driveways over 6,000 sqft — battery limitations mean multiple charge cycles for big properties
  • Icy conditions — like any snow blower, struggles with pure ice

The Yarbo Is the Real Deal — With One Important Caveat

For Connecticut homeowners who are tired of snow removal and willing to invest $4,999 upfront, the Yarbo delivers something no other product currently offers: genuine, hands-off autonomy through the majority of a New England winter.

It handles the 20–22 typical CT snow events per season extremely well. It wakes up automatically. It clears before you need to leave for work. It handles wet, heavy New England snow better than single-stage electrics. And it's built to industrial standards that should last 10+ years.

The honest caveat: it is not a gas blower replacement for extreme events. If you get a true Nor'easter dropping 20+ inches, you may still need a backup. But those storms are rare — maybe 1–3 times per decade in CT. For everything else, the Yarbo handles it while you sleep.

If your budget stretches and winter snow removal is a genuine pain point in your life — this is the most compelling smart yard investment available in 2026.
