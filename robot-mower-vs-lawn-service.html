Lawn service pricing varies by yard size, provider, and region — but here's what we found from surveying local services across Hartford County, including South Windsor, Manchester, Glastonbury, and Vernon:

Standard mow + edge (medium yard) $55–$75 18–22 $990–$1,650
Full service (large yard >½ acre) $80–$120 18–22 $1,440–$2,640

Most Connecticut homeowners with a standard suburban yard (roughly 6,000–10,000 sqft) land in the $1,100–$1,600/year range for basic mowing alone. Add fertilizing, overseeding, and aeration and you're easily at $1,800–$2,500 per year.

⚠️ Don't Forget These prices typically increase 5–8% per year. A service costing $1,200/year today will cost closer to $1,600–$1,750 in five years at that rate.

When a Lawn Service Still Makes Sense

In the spirit of honest reviews, here's when sticking with a lawn service is the smarter call:

✅ Robot Mower Wins When
  • Your yard is under 1 acre
  • Terrain is relatively flat or moderate slope
  • You want to set-and-forget
  • You're paying $800+/year for mowing
  • You want a quieter, eco-friendly option
  • You travel frequently
✗ Lawn Service Wins When
  • Yard is over 1.5 acres (most models struggle)
  • Very steep slopes over 45°
  • Lots of obstacles, trees, tight spaces
  • You also need edging, trimming, cleanup
  • You're only paying under $600/year
  • You rent and move frequently
📍 Connecticut-Specific Note New England yards present one real challenge: fall leaves. Robot mowers aren't leaf blowers — you'll still need to handle leaf cleanup in October/November. Factor that in. Some homeowners keep a minimal leaf-cleanup service ($150–200/season) and drop the full mowing contract.
